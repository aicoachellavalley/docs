## Per-listing page is the goldmine

A single `/listing/{slug}/{id}/` fetch returns:

- Clean schema.org JSON-LD with `@type` (`TouristAttraction`, `Restaurant`, `LodgingBusiness`, etc.)
- `name`, `telephone`, `streetAddress`, `addressLocality`, `geo` coordinates, `description`, primary image URL
- No outbound `partner_website` field in the JSON-LD itself — that may live in the HTML body as a "Visit Website" button. Needs confirmation in a future spike.

This means **per-listing crawl can build the full inventory directly from the sitemap**, with `@type` substituting for VGPS's category labels.

## Implications for Phase 1 design

### Locked
- Drop category-index crawling for the inventory step — it doesn't yield listings in static HTML.
- Use sitemap as the canonical URL set. Per-listing crawl extracts identity + JSON-LD `@type`.
- Robots crawl-delay is 2s. ~4,472 listings × 2s = ~2.5 hours just to fetch inventory. Acceptable, but plan for it.
- Confirm partner-website extraction works on a 5-listing spike before committing to the full 4,472 crawl.

### Needs Sat's call
1. **Category labels — pick one:**
   - (a) Use schema.org `@type` only. Simplest. Loses VGPS's nuance (e.g., `boutique-hotels-and-inns` vs `luxury-hotels` both become `LodgingBusiness` / `Hotel`).
   - (b) Render category index pages via headless browser to harvest VGPS labels. Adds ~30 category-page renders + listing-to-category mapping. Recovers full nuance.
   - (c) Hybrid — sitemap + `@type` for the long tail, headless render of the ~3 most strategic category roots (Hotels, Restaurants, Wellness) to label those.

2. **Vacation rentals — pick one:**
   - (a) Include all. Inventory ~4,472. Phase 2 sample includes rentals.
   - (b) Exclude vacation rentals entirely. Inventory drops to ~3,500-3,800. More aligned with "partner businesses" spirit of the audit. Phase 2 stays focused on the businesses AICV would actually engage.
   - (c) Include but bucket separately. Wide scan still runs the rubric on them; sample selection treats them as their own category.

3. **Phase 2 scope, given the scale revision:**
   - Spec was ~150 partners × 4 systems × 10 queries = 6,000 queries.
   - At ~3,500 businesses × 11/category × 30 categories the math doesn't hold — Phase 2 would never finish.
   - Realistic options:
     - Hard cap Phase 2 sample at 111 total (founding alignment), stratified across top ~10 strategic categories. Skip the long tail.
     - OR shrink Phase 2 to 3 systems × 5 queries × stratified sample, as Sat already flagged on the Chrome MCP question.

### Suggested next step
Spike: fetch 10 sample listings (5 hotels + 5 restaurants + a few attractions) via per-listing GET to confirm partner-website extraction reliability and JSON-LD field coverage. That validates the inventory plan before the 2.5-hour full crawl.
