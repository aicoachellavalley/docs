## Anomalies the run surfaced (the genuinely surprising stuff)

### 1. Metacategory taxonomy is much wider than the spike showed

The spike found 5 metacategories. The run is finding **17+ distinct uppercase-only `catname` values** in the first 125 records alone:

```
EAT & DRINK · STAY · MEETING & EVENT RESOURCES · GETTING HERE & AROUND
SHOPPING · RELOCATION & RESOURCES · THINGS TO DO · GROUP VENUES
GETTING HERE · ARTS & CULTURE · WEDDINGS · BUSINESS & PROFESSIONAL SERVICES
CASINOS & ENTERTAINMENT · PLACES TO STAY · ACTIVITIES & RECREATION · TOURS
(plus 15 records with no metacategory at all)
```

**Apparent label-duplication problems:**
- `STAY` (21) vs `PLACES TO STAY` (3) — same bucket, two labels?
- `GETTING HERE` (5) vs `GETTING HERE & AROUND` (8) — same bucket, two labels?
- `THINGS TO DO`, `ACTIVITIES & RECREATION`, `TOURS`, `CASINOS & ENTERTAINMENT` all sit in roughly the same conceptual space

The 6-bucket framing in the courier prompt assumed a cleaner VGPS taxonomy than reality. **Decision needed:** how to reconcile.

### 2. Vacation-rental slug-prune missed major Airbnb-style leaks

Anomalies report flagged these survivors:
- `2Br Golfside Getaway in Desert Falls` (smartervacation.rentals)
- `3/BDR Private Oasis Pool|Spa` (airbnb.com)
- `3bd Modern Home in Gated Comm. 1mi To Festivals` (airbnb.com)
- `3BR Spanish Townhome` (airbnb.com)
- `4Bed/2Bath Private Saltwater Pool`
- `5 Palms Estate`, `6 Bedroom Luxury in Coachella`, `7 Bedrooms-7.5 Baths`
- `A+ Privacy! 49-Jet Spa` (airbnb.com)

These slugs don't match the `hosted-by` / `acme-house` / `vacasa` patterns. They're individual airbnb listings VGPS apparently imports wholesale. **A weburl-host-based exclusion at Inventory** (airbnb.com, vrbo.com, vacasa.com, smartervacation.rentals, etc.) would catch them. The sub-category exclusion didn't catch them either — their VGPS sub-category appears to be just "Hotels|Resorts" or similar, not labeled as rentals.

### 3. Multi-listing entities, confirmed and worse than expected

Same business under multiple metacategories with different vgps_ids:
- `Acrisure Arena` → MEETING & EVENT RESOURCES + GROUP VENUES
- `Ace Hotel & Swim Club` → STAY + WEDDINGS + GROUP VENUES (3 listings)
- `533 Viet Fusion` → EAT & DRINK + WEDDINGS + GROUP VENUES (3 listings)
- `360 Sports` → 3 listings under EAT & DRINK at different casino properties
- `A Transportation Service, LLC` → WEDDINGS + GETTING HERE & AROUND

**Implication:** without dedup by `weburl`, the same business gets scored multiple times (which IS fine for rubric stats — it's the same homepage) but it also inflates per-metacategory totals and skews tier-by-metacategory cuts. **Decision needed:** dedup strategy.

### 4. ~13% of records have no weburl

16 of the first 125 records had `weburl: null`. Examples named in the aggregate anomalies: `29 Palms Spa & Nails`, `4 Seasons at Desert Breezes`, `A Fox Salon`, `ACE Parking`. These are legitimate businesses with VGPS listings but no outbound URL. They'd all auto-Tier-D in the rubric. **Question:** are they worth keeping in the corpus, or should they be moved to a separate "no-web-presence" bucket so they don't drag the D count?

- **Kill it** — clean slate, but we lose 3 hours of fetching that's already in flight.
- **Let it run while we discuss** — same as option 1 but no commitment to keep it.
