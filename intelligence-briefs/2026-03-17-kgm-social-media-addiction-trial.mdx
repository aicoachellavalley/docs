# Social Media Addiction Trial Enters Jury Deliberations — Coachella Valley Has a Stake

**Date:** March 17, 2026

## Signal

KGM v. Meta Platforms and YouTube LLC entered jury deliberations on March 14, 2026, at the Los Angeles Superior Court — the first jury trial over social media addiction claims in U.S. history. Closing arguments concluded March 12–13 after six weeks of testimony. TikTok and Snap Inc. settled before trial; Meta and YouTube are the remaining defendants. For a plaintiff verdict, at least nine of twelve jurors must find that either company's design was a substantial factor in causing harm.

## Context

The plaintiff, a 20-year-old identified as KGM, alleges that early use of YouTube beginning at age 6 and Instagram beginning at age 9 contributed to compulsive use, body dysmorphia, depression, and suicidal ideation. Internal company documents introduced at trial showed that engagement mechanics — infinite scroll, autoplay, notification loops — were understood by both companies to affect adolescent behavior. Meta CEO Mark Zuckerberg testified before the jury on February 18, 2026, the first jury testimony of his career. Meta's defense centered on pre-existing family circumstances and the absence of a social media diagnosis in KGM's therapy records.

The trial is designated a bellwether, meaning its outcome will directly shape litigation strategy across more than 10,000 individual pending cases and approximately 800 school district claims nationwide. A plaintiff verdict could pressure both companies toward platform redesign; a defense verdict would significantly reduce that pressure.

The Coachella Valley connection is concrete. Seniors represent approximately 20% of the valley's population. The platform design mechanics at issue in the trial — AI-assisted recommendation engines, algorithmic amplification, autoplay — are the same vectors through which AI-generated deepfakes and misinformation now propagate to older users. The trial makes the underlying design question legally explicit: is platform-level AI engineering that maximizes engagement a product liability risk?
