# Navan Edge Signals the Agent-to-Agent Travel Era

**Date:** March 2, 2026

## Signal

Navan, a corporate travel and expense platform, launched Navan Edge on March 2 — an AI-powered assistant designed to give every business traveler personalized support previously reserved for executives with dedicated staff. The product targets the unmanaged travel segment: frequent travelers whose employers either lack a corporate booking platform or have one too cumbersome to use, resulting in employees booking outside it. Navan estimates this segment at roughly $56 billion in total addressable market. CEO Ariel Cohen framed it directly: travel is a top use case for LLMs, and consumers want to say the word and let AI handle the rest — but that conversational interface must operate on top of infrastructure connecting hundreds of suppliers.

## Agent Signal

Corporate travel agents will query supplier infrastructure to book Coachella Valley retreats. Hotels, venues, and activity providers not represented in structured, agent-readable formats will be invisible to Navan-class systems. Node Zero is the routing layer that makes AICV's node inventory queryable by these agents.

## Context

Navan Edge is a proof point for AICV's core thesis. The group and corporate travel use case — retreats, conferences, executive offsites — is one of three primary agent use cases AICV is building toward, alongside economic development and tourism relocation. The scenario is no longer hypothetical: an agent representing a company's booking team will be dispatched to find hotels, flights, transportation, venues, dining, and activities for a Coachella Valley retreat, negotiate with supplier agents, and complete bookings autonomously. Navan Edge is an early commercial form of that agent. The intelligence layer AICV is building — structured, machine-readable nodes for resorts, venues, and services across 9 cities — is precisely the kind of supplier infrastructure Navan's CEO says is required for this conversational interface to function. Hotels, retreat venues, and activity providers in the valley that are not represented in structured, agent-readable formats will be invisible to these systems. Node Zero, AICV's routing and concierge layer currently in development, is the direct response to this moment.
