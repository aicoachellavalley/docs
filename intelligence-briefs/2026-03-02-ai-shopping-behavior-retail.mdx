# AI Becomes the Default Shopping Layer — Local Retail Has a Narrow Window

**Date:** March 2, 2026

## Signal

A Synchrony survey released March 2 found that more than half of American consumers used generative AI tools while shopping during the 2025 holiday season — a threshold that marks the shift from novelty to mainstream behavior. Among AI users, 34% used it to compare products and 29% used it to find the best price. Adobe Analytics separately reported that AI platform referral traffic to retail websites surged 693% year over year during November and December 2025. Shoppers arriving via AI tools converted at a rate 31% higher than those from other channels and were 33% less likely to bounce. Generational split: 45% of Gen Z is comfortable receiving AI product recommendations; only 25% of baby boomers share that comfort. 41% of Gen Z expects to use an AI agent to complete shopping tasks on their behalf in the future. eTail West, held in Palm Desert this season, surfaced no significant local or national retail models that address this structural shift.

## Context

The Coachella Valley's retail concentration on El Paseo — currently transitioning to Simon Property Group ownership — sits at the intersection of two pressures. The first is the consumer behavior shift already underway: shoppers are arriving at purchase decisions through AI conversations, not search. Retailers not represented in AI-readable formats are losing discovery before a human ever visits their website. The second pressure is the agent-commerce transition still 2-3 years out, in which AI agents complete purchases autonomously on behalf of consumers. National chains on El Paseo have parent infrastructure that may adapt. Independent and regional retailers face the transition without support. AICV's El Paseo node documents this corridor as a structured intelligence asset. The Simon Property Group ownership transition represents a potential opening — new ownership, new urgency — to work with merchants on AI discoverability and agent-readiness before the 2026 holiday season. The window is narrow. Adobe's 693% referral surge did not happen gradually. It happened in one season.
