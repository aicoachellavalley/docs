# CSUSB Randall W. Lewis Center Crosses $1 Billion in Regional Economic Impact

**Date:** March 13, 2026

## Signal

Cal State San Bernardino's Randall W. Lewis Center for Entrepreneurship announced it has generated \$1.013 billion in cumulative regional economic impact — ahead of internal projections. The milestone spans approximately 23 years and covers 213,000 businesses assisted, 2,800 startups launched, and 62,000 jobs created or retained across the Inland Empire. Director Mike Stull credited the Entrepreneurial Resource Center network — with locations in San Bernardino, Temecula, and Palm Desert — as the initiative that most significantly expanded the center's reach. The announcement made no reference to AI.

## Context

Annualized, the \$1.013 billion figure averages approximately \$44 million per year in economic impact — a meaningful number for a nonprofit, though less headline-ready than the cumulative total. Across 213,000 businesses over 23 years, the per-business average impact works out to roughly \$4,800, suggesting the program delivered substantive support to a large base of participants, with outsize returns concentrated among a smaller cohort of high-growth businesses. Standard startup failure rates — approximately 50% within five years nationally, closer to one in five in California — mean a portion of the counted businesses are no longer operating, though their revenue contributions remain in the cumulative figure.

No geographic breakdown by subregion was included in the announcement. Coachella Valley represents approximately 10.6% of the Inland Empire by population — a rough extrapolation suggests somewhere around \$107 million of the total impact may be attributable to the valley, though the ERC's Palm Desert presence is more recent than the program's full 23-year span. The real number, specific to the Coachella Valley, has not been published.

AI Coachella Valley operates from the ERC on Cook Street in Palm Desert and maintains an active partnership with the center. The goal is to meaningfully grow the Coachella Valley's contribution to the region's startup and entrepreneurial output — with a specific focus on what that looks like in the age of AI. The valley's potential share of future impact, not the historical average, is where that work is aimed.
