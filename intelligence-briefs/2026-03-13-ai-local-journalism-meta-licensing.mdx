# AI Licensing Deals Leave Local Journalism Behind

**Date:** March 13, 2026

## Signal

Large media corporations — including News Corp, CNN, USA Today, and Le Monde — are entering licensing agreements with Meta, receiving millions annually for content that trains Meta AI across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Messenger. Local and community journalists are excluded from these deals. Their bylined work still feeds the training pipeline, but revenue flows to parent corporations rather than newsrooms or writers. AI-generated summaries are simultaneously reducing click-through traffic to publisher websites, compressing the advertising and subscription revenue that funds reporter salaries.

## Context

The dynamic mirrors earlier platform dependency cycles — Google in the 2000s, Facebook in the 2010s — where publishers traded reach for traffic, only to find leverage shift entirely to the platform. For local news outlets, the math is more severe: a percentage-point drop in click-through represents a proportionally larger hit on a smaller revenue base.

In the Inland Empire, the Inland Empire Journalism Hub and Fund represents one of the more significant institutional efforts to sustain local reporting across Riverside and San Bernardino Counties. Their work is concentrated in the broader region — Coachella Valley coverage remains limited in that footprint. AI Coachella Valley and SunshineFM operate as the valley's AI-forward presence in this landscape — maintaining active engagement with regional journalism organizations as the structural questions around platform dependency, licensing revenue, and local editorial sustainability play out in real time.

The open question across the industry is whether AI fatigue eventually creates demand for premium human-reported, locally sourced content — material agents cannot synthesize because it was never published in a form they could train on. If that shift occurs, community publishers who survived the transition intact would hold a structural advantage.
