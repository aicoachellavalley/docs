# KESQ Cites Coachella Valley Responsible AI Framework in Iran Deepfakes Story

**Date:** March 10, 2026

## Signal

KESQ reporters contacted AICV on March 10 seeking comment on AI use in reporting the Iran conflict. The conversation was redirected toward responsible AI principles — specifically the Pledge for Responsible AI published at aicoachellavalley.org — rather than political or military analysis. A segment is expected to air March 11.

## Context

The Iran conflict has generated a documented flood of AI-generated deepfake footage across social media platforms. A South Korean outlet reported fabricated scenes including strikes on the USS Lincoln and mushroom clouds over Tehran, with artifacts including frozen smoke, duplicated faces, and at least one visible Google Gemini image generation watermark embedded in a published photo. University of South Florida experts have described the conflict as a demonstration of how AI enables propaganda at a scale and sophistication that exceeds anything seen in prior conflicts.

A compounding effect has emerged: AI skepticism is now causing authentic footage to be dismissed alongside fakes, degrading the information environment independent of which specific videos are real. The White House separately released edited content blending real strike footage with video game aesthetics and slogans including "Flawless Victory" — a format experts describe as gamified spectacle that frames real violence as entertainment and erodes the public's capacity for sober democratic debate.

Two principles from the AICV Pledge for Responsible AI are directly implicated: truthfulness and transparency, both of which require that AI-generated or AI-modified content be clearly labeled. The Pledge is the active curriculum for AICV's 2025–2026 student workshop program across the nine cities of the Coachella Valley. The next workshop is scheduled for the Big Brothers Big Sisters summit on March 21 at Classic Club in Palm Desert.
