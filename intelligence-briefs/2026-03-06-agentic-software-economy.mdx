# Jensen Huang Declares All Future Software Will Be Agentic

**Date:** March 6, 2026

## Signal

Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang, speaking at the Morgan Stanley Technology Conference in San Francisco on March 5–6, 2026, stated that all future software will be agentic, framing static software as obsolete. Huang confirmed Nvidia has finalized a \$30 billion investment in OpenAI and a \$10 billion stake in Anthropic — characterizing both as likely the final private investments before each company's IPO. Separately, Box CEO Aaron Levie stated this week that AI agents are now his company's primary clientele, not humans, and projected the addressable software market could grow tenfold as a result.

## Context

Huang's thesis reframes the entire software industry: today's seat-based licensing model — where companies pay per employee login across platforms like Salesforce, Microsoft, and HubSpot — faces structural displacement as AI agents begin performing knowledge work directly. The emerging model bills for AI labor by the token, not by the seat. Huang estimated agentic AI will generate 1,000 times more compute demand than conventional AI chatbots, with agents operating 24/7 rather than on human schedules. For the Coachella Valley, this shift has direct workforce implications: the knowledge workers most exposed to agentic displacement — customer service, administrative, paralegal, accounting — represent a significant share of the valley's white-collar employment base. The valley's current AI workforce readiness infrastructure is nascent. No major employer in the region has publicly disclosed an agentic deployment strategy as of this date.
