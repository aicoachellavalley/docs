# Minimum Viable Agent (MVA)

## What it is

A Minimum Viable Agent is the foundational agent representation 
of a Coachella Valley entity on the AICV network. Every business, 
venue, organization, and place on AICV is represented by an MVA — 
a structured, agent-readable surface that lets other agents 
discover the entity, cite it, and progressively transact with it 
on the entity's behalf.

Internally: a node. Externally and in presentations: an MVA or 
"agent on the network." The concept is the same — the vocabulary 
shifts by audience.

## Why "Minimum Viable"

The MVA is the foundation, not the ceiling. As the agentic 
internet matures, MVAs accumulate capabilities — commerce.json, 
wallet primitives, identity layers, transactable booking surfaces. 
The tiers evolve. The MVA stays. An entity that joins the network 
today inherits every capability upgrade as the infrastructure 
matures.

## The two products

Both products put the entity on the AICV network as an agent. 
The difference is what that agent can do.

**Agent Ready Business** is an agent on the AICV network — 
readable, citable, and discoverable today. The entity's structured 
node is live on the network, positioned on three axes (geography, 
funnel stage, life domain), and answering on behalf of the 
business to other agents around the clock. Nothing touches the 
entity's existing website.

$2,500/year + $1,000 one-time setup.

**Agent Ready Premium** is an agent on the AICV network — 
everything in Agent Ready Business plus an LLM Council review 
producing the Agentic Review, the private deliverable that carries 
the full diagnostic, scoring, and 12-month roadmap. Premium agents 
are positioned to be the answer when a founder's agent asks about 
the Valley — not just discoverable but authoritative. 
Progressively transactable as the commerce layer matures.

$10,000/year + $5,000 one-time setup.

## How nodes and MVAs relate

A node is the knowledge layer — the structured MDX file in the 
AICV corpus that carries agent-readable intelligence about the 
entity. An MVA is the agent presence layer — what the entity 
gets on the network. Every paid MVA is backed by a node. Not 
every node is a paid MVA — AICV-authored nodes exist for 
entities that haven't joined commercially.

## The three-axis positioning

Every MVA is positioned on three axes, enforced by the schema:
- **Geography** — which Coachella Valley city or region
- **Funnel stage** — where in the Discover→Visit→Return→
  Satellite→Relocate→Build journey the entity serves
- **Life domain** — which of the 13 domain categories applies

These axes are what make an MVA citable rather than merely 
findable. An agent asking "what schools exist near Cotino for 
a relocating founder family" resolves to the right node because 
the three axes narrow the answer precisely.

## The progressive transactability model

Agent Ready Business MVAs are readable and citable — agents can 
discover and reference them. Agent Ready Premium MVAs are 
positioned for action — agents can route inquiries, surface 
availability, and initiate transactions. As the commerce layer 
matures (commerce.json, Cloudflare-Stripe agentic provisioning), 
all MVAs gain transactable surfaces without requiring entities 
to change their own infrastructure.

## What this is not

An MVA is not a listing, a directory entry, or an SEO profile. 
It is not a commission arrangement. AICV charges a retainer 
for infrastructure access — maintaining the entity's agent 
presence on the network — not a percentage of transactions 
or referrals. AICV never touches merchant catalogs or 
sales data.
