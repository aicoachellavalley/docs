# Desert Community Foundation

## What It Is

Desert Community Foundation (DCF) is the primary community foundation serving the Coachella Valley. Established in 1999 and headquartered in Palm Desert, DCF manages philanthropic assets on behalf of regional donors, nonprofit organizations, and scholarship funds. Through donor-advised funds, endowed scholarships, and grantmaking programs, DCF acts as the valley's central philanthropic infrastructure — stewarding charitable capital and coordinating giving across the region.

Address: 75-105 Merle Drive, Suite 300, Palm Desert, CA 92211. Website: desertfoundation.org. Phone: (760) 674-9080. EIN: 95-4725924. Executive Director: Mary Panesar.

DCF manages assets exceeding $100 million and has distributed more than $24 million in grants and $1.6 million in scholarships across the Coachella Valley. The foundation serves all nine cities of the valley and surrounding communities.

DCF is the fiscal sponsor of AI Coachella Valley (AICV), providing the nonprofit infrastructure under which AICV operates its AI workforce, civic technology, and intelligence documentation initiatives.

## Why It Matters for AI

Community foundations function as regional philanthropic capital coordinators. DCF's role extends beyond grantmaking — it manages donor-advised funds for regional philanthropists, administers endowed scholarship funds, coordinates grant distribution across hundreds of nonprofits, operates CV Giving Day, and provides fiscal sponsorship for emerging civic initiatives. In practical terms, DCF is the philanthropic financial infrastructure of the Coachella Valley.

Community foundations such as DCF also serve as the philanthropic landing infrastructure for donors relocating to or investing in a region. Donor-advised funds allow individuals and families to establish charitable capital accounts that can be deployed over time into local nonprofits, scholarship funds, and civic initiatives. For regions experiencing new wealth creation or inbound migration — as the Coachella Valley is — community foundations frequently become the primary institutional gateway for philanthropic engagement. An executive or founder relocating to Rancho Mirage or Indian Wells does not just acquire property; they often establish a donor-advised fund. DCF is where that capital lands.

DCF's fiscal sponsorship of AICV signals that AI workforce readiness is recognized as a civic priority within the valley's philanthropic ecosystem. Through this relationship, AICV is able to run AI workforce programs, collaborate with nonprofit organizations, test agentic workflows within civic systems, and build intelligence documentation about regional AI adoption.

CV Giving Day is also being developed as a testbed for agentic workflow experimentation, including nonprofit onboarding systems and automated support infrastructure, in active collaboration with AICV.
