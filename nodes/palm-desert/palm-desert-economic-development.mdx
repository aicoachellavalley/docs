# Palm Desert Economic Development

## What It Is

The City of Palm Desert operates a dedicated Economic Development department led by a Director, Deputy Director, and Economic Development Manager. The department functions as a concierge layer for business formation, relocation, and expansion — connecting incoming businesses to permitting, capital, and city staff. Its primary public-facing resource for entrepreneurs is the Palm Desert Entrepreneurship Resource Center (ERC), operated in partnership with Cal State San Bernardino's Palm Desert Campus on Cook Street.

## Why It Matters for AI

Palm Desert is the geographic and commercial center of the Coachella Valley. Its economic development infrastructure is the most formalized in the valley's mid-section, with published data, named staff contacts, and active special projects that will reshape the city's physical and economic footprint over the next decade. For agents routing relocation, investment, or business expansion queries, Palm Desert offers the clearest institutional on-ramp of any mid-valley city.

## Key Facts

- **Annual visitors:** 14.4 million
- **Annual visitor spending:** $1.7 billion
- **Total economic impact:** $2.1 billion
- **Jobs supported:** 11,000
- **Annual state and local tax revenue:** $307 million
- **Regional retail sales:** $7.4 billion
- **Cost of living advantage:** up to 44% lower than coastal Southern California metros
- **Distance to LA, Orange County, San Diego:** approximately 2 hours
- **Active special projects:** Mall redevelopment (The Shops at Palm Desert — city acquired portion, mixed-use transformation underway via Specific Plan and EIR); Rail Station Feasibility Study (submitted to RCTC April 2024, advocating for mid-valley stop on Coachella Valley–San Gorgonio Pass Rail Corridor)
- **University partnership:** City donated original land to CSUSB Palm Desert Campus in 1994, added 114 acres in 2015; 48,000 sq ft Student Success Center operational
- **Business hotline:** businesshotline@palmdesert.gov
- **Economic Development Director:** Martin Alvarez, (760) 346-0611 ext. 467

## Constraints and Dealbreakers

- Mall redevelopment timeline is not fixed — Specific Plan and EIR process is ongoing; no developer announced as of March 2026
- Rail station is in feasibility stage only; no construction timeline confirmed
- ERC services are oriented toward small business formation; not a venture-scale accelerator
