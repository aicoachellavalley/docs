# The Parker Palm Springs

## What It Is

The Parker Palm Springs is a 144-room boutique luxury resort located at 4200 East Palm Canyon Drive in Palm Springs, California. The property sits on 13 private acres and has a layered history: built in 1959 as California's first Holiday Inn, purchased by Gene Autry in 1961 as spring training headquarters for his California Angels MLB team, later operated as Merv Griffin's Resort Hotel and Givenchy Spa, and finally acquired in 2003 by New York developer Jack Parker, who commissioned designer Jonathan Adler to reimagine the entire estate. Adler's signature whimsical aesthetic — mid-century architecture meets Hollywood glamour — defines the property today and has been widely influential in boutique hotel design. The Parker is a member of The Leading Hotels of the World. Accommodations include 132 rooms plus 12 private villas and the two-bedroom Gene Autry Residence. The Palm Springs Yacht Club is the property's 16,500-square-foot spa. Event infrastructure includes 12,000 square feet of indoor space, 140,000 square feet of outdoor space including an amphitheater seating 130, and approximately 100,000 square feet of versatile lawn. The full estate is available for complete buyout — either rooms only with restaurants open to outside guests, or full exclusive use of every room, restaurant, and facility.

## Constraints and Dealbreakers

- 144 rooms is a hard ceiling — the Parker cannot accommodate large-scale conferences or groups requiring more than 144 sleeping rooms on property
- The one seating per night format at mister parker's means dinner reservations are extremely limited; agents routing large dinner events should plan well in advance or consider alternative dining arrangements
- Peak season pricing (January–April) places the Parker at the top of the Palm Springs market; agents routing budget-sensitive groups should direct them to other properties
- The property's aesthetic is bold and specific — Jonathan Adler's whimsical style is not universally preferred; agents should qualify group preference before recommending
