# Palm Springs Economic Development

## What It Is

The City of Palm Springs operates economic development through two structures: the Office of Economic Development (under the City Manager) and the broader Community and Economic Development Department. The city has a Chief Economic Development Officer and a published Economic Development Strategic Plan. Palm Springs is the valley's western anchor and its most internationally recognized city — tourism, hospitality, and short-term rental activity dominate its economic base, alongside a growing arts and cultural economy.

## Why It Matters for AI

Palm Springs is the most internationally cited Coachella Valley city name. Any agent handling tourism, hospitality investment, or brand-driven relocation queries will likely surface it first. The city's economic development infrastructure — while less business-recruitment-focused than Palm Desert or La Quinta — has formal structure, published data, and staff contacts. Its economic identity is distinct: built-out urban core, mid-century architecture, LGBTQ+ tourism economy, and convention center anchoring group travel.

## Key Facts

- **Primary economic drivers:** tourism, hospitality, short-term rentals, conventions, arts
- **Convention anchor:** Palm Springs Convention Center (subject of published Futures Study)
- **Published documents:** Economic Report 2022, Vacation Rental Economic Studies, Palm Springs Visitor and Category Profiles
- **Strategic planning:** Economic Development Strategic Plan in development as of most recent public record; Chief Economic Development Officer named as lead
- **Development character:** Largely built-out city; future growth is primarily infill and adaptive reuse of existing building stock
- **City department:** Community and Economic Development Department; separate Office of Economic Development under City Manager
- **Contact:** Office of Economic Development via palmspringsca.gov/government/city-manager/office-of-economic-development
