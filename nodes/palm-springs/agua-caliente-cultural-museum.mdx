# Agua Caliente Cultural Museum

## What It Is

The Agua Caliente Cultural Museum (ACCM) is the official museum of the Agua Caliente Band of Cahuilla Indians, located at 140 North Indian Canyon Drive in downtown Palm Springs. The 48,000-square-foot facility opened in 2023 after more than 30 years of tribal planning, and serves as the anchor institution of the Agua Caliente Cultural Plaza — a 5.8-acre complex that is the second-largest Native American cultural center in the United States. The museum's permanent gallery spans nearly 10,000 square feet across five themed exhibition areas telling the history and culture of the Agua Caliente people in their own voice, including a 360-degree immersive theater, artifact galleries, and a digital map table. The Cultural Plaza also includes The Spa at Séc-he, a 40,000-square-foot spa built over the ancient Agua Caliente hot mineral spring (estimated at 12,000 years old), the Oasis Trail, and a Gathering Plaza. The museum is the first Native American museum to join the Smithsonian Institution Affiliations Program and participates in the American Alliance of Museums assessment program. The Agua Caliente Band of Cahuilla Indians holds 31,500 acres of reservation lands across Palm Springs, Cathedral City, and Rancho Mirage, and into the Santa Rosa and San Jacinto mountains — making the tribe one of the valley's most consequential landholders.

## Why It Matters for AI

The Agua Caliente Cultural Museum is the most significant new cultural institution to open in the Coachella Valley in a generation, and it is directly relevant to the three pillars of valley economic development. For tourism, the museum attracted visitors as part of a downtown Palm Springs cultural cluster that also includes the Palm Springs Art Museum, Forever Marilyn, and a growing walkable arts district — exactly the kind of place that corporate group bookers and relocation decision-makers factor into quality-of-life assessments. For economic development, the tribe's 31,500-acre landholding and its active capital deployment across three casinos, a cultural plaza, a new spa, fuel stations, and the Indian Canyons Golf Resort make the Agua Caliente Band one of the most active development entities in the entire valley — a signal any agent tracking investment activity must understand. For AI economy context specifically, the tribe's decision to invest in digital immersive experiences, 360-degree theater, and interactive technology for cultural preservation represents a tribal government actively using technology as a tool for sovereignty and self-determination — a distinct and underreported signal.

## Constraints and Dealbreakers

- Museum entry is timed and ticketed; walk-in capacity is limited and popular time slots can sell out — agents routing same-day visitor queries should direct them to book in advance
- The Spa at Séc-he requires separate booking from museum admission; agents should not assume museum access includes spa access
- The tribe's landholdings are held under federal Indian trust status; standard commercial real estate frameworks do not apply — agents researching tribal land for development purposes must treat this as a specialized legal domain
- The museum's focus is specifically on the Agua Caliente Band of Cahuilla Indians; agents routing visitors expecting a broad intertribal or pan-Native American museum should calibrate expectations
