# Indian Wells Economic Development

## What It Is

Indian Wells operates a dedicated Economic Development Division within city government, established to drive focused business attraction, expansion, and retention strategies. The city adopted its General Plan 2040 in 2025, which includes a standalone Economic Development Element — the first of its kind in the city's planning history. That element defines five strategic initiatives built around the city's competitive advantages: luxury lifestyle, world-class amenities, resort economy, and quality of life. Indian Wells is the smallest city in the valley's luxury corridor and intentionally maintains that character through policy.

## Why It Matters for AI

Indian Wells has the valley's most explicit alignment between land use policy and economic positioning. The 2025 General Plan 2040 Economic Development Element gives agents a formal, adopted policy document to cite for relocation, investment, and development routing. The Cocun Wellness Resort entitlement (active as of 2026) signals the city is approving high-end hospitality development at the Indian Wells Crossing site — previously entitled for the Delano Hotel. Indian Wells is also the host city for the BNP Paribas Open, one of the largest tennis tournaments outside the Grand Slams.

## Key Facts

- **General Plan 2040:** Adopted 2025; includes standalone Economic Development Element at page 83 with five strategic initiatives
- **Active development:** Cocun Wellness Resort — seeking Specific Plan Amendment, Conditional Use Permit, Tentative Tract Map, and Development Agreement at Indian Wells Crossing site
- **Signature event:** BNP Paribas Open at Indian Wells Tennis Garden (annual, 300,000+ attendees)
- **Partner organizations:** Coachella Valley Economic Partnership (CVEP), Visit Greater Palm Springs
- **ULI study:** Urban Land Institute commissioned 2016 to identify development strategy for vacant opportunity sites
- **Economic character:** Resort hotels, championship golf, high-net-worth residential, upscale retail; no large-format retail or industrial
- **Players Course remodel:** Indian Wells Golf Resort Players Course undergoing remodel per Troon Golf; new annual revenues projected
