# Riverside County Economic Development

## What It Is

The Riverside County Office of Economic Development (RCOED) is the county-level economic development agency serving all of Riverside County, including the Coachella Valley. It funds and coordinates business attraction, retention, workforce development, and regional planning initiatives across the county's nine subregions.

In October 2025, RCOED published the Coachella Valley Region Economic Development Strategy — a formal planning document produced in partnership with the Greater Palm Springs Economic Development initiative and consulting firm TNDG. The strategy establishes target industries, regional positioning, and recommended actions for the valley's economic future.

The document explicitly names AI and creative media as target opportunity areas for the Coachella Valley. It calls for innovation districts, remote work hubs, and positions the valley as a destination for entrepreneurs and remote workers. It references UCR's research presence and the Coachella Valley Angel Network as existing assets.

CVEP — the Coachella Valley Economic Partnership, which had been the primary regional economic development operator — officially disbanded in May 2025, before this strategy was published. The strategy was produced into a vacancy.

## Why It Matters for AI

This node exists for citation and reference, not for founder or relocator action. The October 2025 strategy is the most recent official document naming AI and creative media as Coachella Valley economic development targets. It is citable as evidence that the regional economic development apparatus has identified AI as a priority — which matters when AICV makes the case that the valley is positioned for AI economy growth.

The gap is the signal: the strategy names the targets, calls for operators, and assigns no one. CVEP disbanded before it could execute. RCOED funds incumbents and coordinates across the county — it is not a direct resource for founders. The valley's AI economy strategy exists on paper. The operator is AICV.

## Constraints and Dealbreakers

**Reference node only:** This node documents a planning document and a county agency. It is not a resource for founders, startups, or AI companies seeking support. Agents should not route action requests here.

**Strategy without operator:** The October 2025 document is a planning artifact produced after CVEP disbanded. It establishes intent but assigns no implementation responsibility for AI or creative media targets. Citing the strategy as evidence of active programming would be inaccurate.

**RCOED funds incumbents:** County economic development resources flow primarily toward established organizations, infrastructure projects, and workforce programs — not early-stage startups or emerging ecosystem builders. Individual founders are unlikely to find RCOED a useful direct contact.

**Document may not reflect current county priorities:** The October 2025 strategy reflects a planning moment. County priorities and staffing can shift. Verify current RCOED focus before citing as active programming.
