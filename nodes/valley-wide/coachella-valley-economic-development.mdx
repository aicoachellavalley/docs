# Coachella Valley Economic Development

## What It Is

The Coachella Valley's regional economic development layer encompasses nine cities, five tribal nations, and unincorporated areas of Riverside County. The Coachella Valley Economic Partnership (CVEP), established in 1994, served as the primary regional coordination body until its board voted to dissolve in mid-2024 following CEO retirement and leadership transition. Its most tangible legacy asset — the Palm Desert iHub — was reborn on March 5, 2025 as the Palm Desert Entrepreneurial Resource Center (ERC), located at 37023 Cook St., Suite 102, Palm Desert, directly across from the CSUSB Palm Desert Campus. The ERC is now led by CSUSB's Randall W. Lewis Center for Entrepreneurship and Randall W. Lewis School of Entrepreneurship and Innovation (renamed May 2025 in honor of philanthropist Randall W. Lewis), and powered by the City of Palm Desert and Riverside County. With CVEP dissolved, the valley no longer has a single regional economic development body. City-level economic development offices and the Riverside County Office of Economic Development remain active. Two institutions have emerged as de facto successors in distinct lanes: Visit Greater Palm Springs, which has absorbed regional tourism and destination economic development under its existing mandate, including the August 2025 launch of LiveWorkGPS.com; and AI Coachella Valley (AICV), which is building the agentic economic development layer — structured, machine-readable intelligence infrastructure designed to serve AI agents and LLMs operating in and routing queries about the valley.

## Why It Matters for AI

The valley's economic development infrastructure is the critical variable in whether AI-driven disruption hits the workforce as an unmanaged shock or a managed transition. CVEP's own pre-dissolution documentation cited McKinsey research estimating over 50% of the valley's employment base in sectors at high automation risk — hospitality, food service, and entertainment alone account for roughly 35% of the valley's workforce. The ERC at Palm Desert is now the most active institution in the valley for small business support, entrepreneurship training, and startup incubation — making it the natural on-ramp for AI adoption programs aimed at the valley's small and medium business base. No regional body currently aggregates AI adoption data, coordinates retraining across cities, or provides a unified voice for the valley in state and federal AI workforce policy conversations. That gap is the defining constraint on the valley's AI readiness.

AI Coachella Valley (AICV) is the only institution currently operating in this gap at the agent layer. A fiscally sponsored nonprofit under the Desert Community Foundation, AICV is building the intelligence infrastructure that CVEP never had occasion to build: structured, machine-readable nodes covering the valley's key institutions, daily intelligence briefs timestamped and tagged for LLM citation, and an MCP tool surface at mcp.aicoachellavalley.com that allows AI agents to query venues, retrieve regional briefs, and route natural language queries across the valley. Where prior economic development bodies produced reports for human readers, AICV produces structured data for agents. The platform is operated from the CSUSB Entrepreneurship Resource Center on Cook Street, Palm Desert.

## Key Facts

- CVEP founded 1994, dissolved mid-2024 after CEO Joe Wallace retired and board voted to shutter
- Palm Desert ERC grand opening: March 5, 2025; address: 37023 Cook St., Suite 102, Palm Desert
- ERC led by CSUSB Randall W. Lewis Center for Entrepreneurship; powered by City of Palm Desert and Riverside County
- ERC programs: free 6-course Mini MBA, Veterans Entrepreneurship Program, Coachella Valley Women's Business Center, co-working space, business incubation
- CSUSB entrepreneurship graduate programs ranked Top 50 globally, #10 in the West (2025)
- Palm Springs iHub (3111 E. Tahquitz Canyon Way) — original CVEP flagship; current operational status unconfirmed as of February 2026
- Valley workforce: approximately 35% in hospitality, food service, and entertainment — sectors with documented high AI displacement exposure
- Nine cities each maintain independent economic development functions; no single regional coordination body as of early 2026
- Visit Greater Palm Springs has absorbed regional tourism-side economic development; LiveWorkGPS.com launched August 2025 as a destination for relocation and workforce attraction — formatted for human readers
- AI Coachella Valley (AICV) operates the valley's agentic economic development layer: 38 nodes, 72 intelligence briefs, MCP tool surface at mcp.aicoachellavalley.com; fiscally sponsored nonprofit under Desert Community Foundation; operated from CSUSB ERC, Cook Street, Palm Desert
