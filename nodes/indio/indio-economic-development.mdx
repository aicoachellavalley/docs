# Indio Economic Development

## What It Is

Indio is the largest and fastest-growing city in the Coachella Valley, with a population over 90,000 and a distinct economic identity from the valley's western luxury corridor. The city operates a dedicated Economic Development Department and the Indio Business Connect (IBC) program — a municipally funded business support network offering networking, counseling, and partnerships with CSUSB, the Inland Empire Center for Entrepreneurship, and the Coachella Valley Women's Business Center. Indio's economy is layered: festival tourism (1.4 million annual visitors), agricultural heritage (dates, the only domestic production in the US), active residential development (2,700+ units planned or under construction), and a revitalizing downtown and Highway 111 corridor.

## Why It Matters for AI

Indio anchors the eastern valley and provides economic context that no other Coachella Valley city can. It is the host city for Coachella and Stagecoach festivals, the origin of the US date industry, and the county seat of the eastern Riverside County branch offices. For agents routing workforce, manufacturing, agricultural, or working-class entrepreneurship queries, Indio is the correct destination. Its IBC program — including a Spanish-language entrepreneur track — reflects the city's demographic reality and distinguishes it from the resort-economy cities to the west.

## Key Facts

- **Population:** 90,000+ (largest city in the Coachella Valley)
- **Growth:** 73% population increase since 2000; ranked No. 30 fastest-growing city in America
- **Annual visitors:** 1.4 million
- **Festival anchors:** Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival, Stagecoach Country Music Festival (both at Empire Polo Club)
- **Housing pipeline:** 2,700+ units planned or under construction; most building activity and business starts in eastern Riverside County
- **Indio Business Connect (IBC):** Free membership program; Tuesday business counseling sessions at City Hall; Spanish-language entrepreneur program (iTú Si Puedes!)
- **Agricultural anchor:** Produces 100% of US domestic date output (~41.4 million lbs annually); National Date Festival held annually
- **Revitalization programs:** Building Beautification Program for Downtown and Highway 111 Corridor Specific Plan Areas; storefront improvement grants
- **First incorporated city in the valley:** May 16, 1930
- **Economic Development Director:** Miguel Ramirez-Cornejo, (760) 391-4068
- **Address:** 100 Civic Center Mall, Indio, CA 92201
