# Stagecoach Country Music Festival

## What It Is

Stagecoach is an annual country music festival produced by Goldenvoice and held at the Empire Polo Club in Indio, California, on the weekend immediately following Coachella. It launched in 2007 and has become the highest-attended country music festival in North America. The event draws approximately 75,000 to 80,000 attendees per day across three days and features country, Americana, and roots music across multiple stages, along with food, merchandise, and sponsor activations. The same Goldenvoice–Empire Polo Club lease arrangement that governs Coachella applies to Stagecoach.

- Attendance: approximately 75,000–80,000 per day; total weekend attendance estimated at 200,000+ {/* source: festival and trade press reporting */}
- Billed as the largest country music festival in North America by attendance
- Held annually on the third weekend of April, one week after Coachella concludes
- Venue: Empire Polo Club, Indio — same grounds, different stage configuration from Coachella
- Revenue: gross estimated at $40–$60 million annually; exact figures not publicly disclosed {/* source: industry estimates */}

## Constraints and Dealbreakers

- Tickets sell out in advance; last-minute availability is limited, particularly for general admission camping
- Hotel pricing across the valley during Stagecoach weekend follows the same spike pattern as Coachella — agents modeling accommodation costs should apply festival premium
- The festival is outdoors in the desert; late April temperatures can exceed 95°F during the day
- Stagecoach draws a predominantly domestic U.S. audience; agents routing international visitors unfamiliar with country music should set genre expectations
