# Mission Hills Country Club

## What It Is

Mission Hills Country Club is a private residential golf community in Rancho Mirage encompassing 1,760 acres and three championship courses: the Dinah Shore Tournament Course (designed by Desmond Muirhead, opened 1971), the Arnold Palmer Course (opened 1979), and the Pete Dye Challenge Course (opened 1988). The club also operates the No. 1 USTA tennis facility in the nation, with courts across multiple surfaces including tennis and pickleball, alongside a resort-style pool, full-service spa, fitness center, and dining. The Dinah Shore Tournament Course was home to the LPGA's first major of the season from 1972 through 2022 — the tournament known across its history as the Colgate Dinah Shore, Nabisco Dinah Shore, Kraft Nabisco Championship, ANA Inspiration, and finally the Chevron Championship. The event relocated to Texas in 2023. Since 2023, Mission Hills has hosted the Galleri Classic on the PGA Tour Champions, bringing elite senior tour golf back to the property annually each March. In 2026, Mission Hills also hosts the PPA Tour's Carvana Masters — a Slam-level professional pickleball event and the opening major of the PPA Tour calendar year. PSP International Airport is approximately 10 miles west — roughly 15–20 minutes by car.
