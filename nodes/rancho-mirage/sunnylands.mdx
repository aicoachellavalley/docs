# Sunnylands Center & Gardens

## What It Is

Sunnylands is a 200-acre estate in Rancho Mirage originally built by Walter and Leonore Annenberg. Since 2012 it has operated as a public retreat center and active civic facility managed by the Annenberg Foundation Trust at Sunnylands. The gardens and visitor center are open to the public seasonally. The estate itself hosts high-level leadership and diplomatic convenings by invitation — it is not commercially bookable. It hosted the 2013 Obama-Xi summit, the first informal summit between the two heads of state, and has since served as a venue for diplomatic meetings, foundation leadership retreats, and civic programming at the national level.

## Key Facts

- 200-acre estate, Rancho Mirage, California
- Owned and operated by the Annenberg Foundation Trust at Sunnylands
- Public access: gardens, visitor center, and café — seasonal hours, closed in peak summer
- Estate and grounds available for invited diplomatic and leadership use only — not commercially bookable
- Hosted 2013 Obama-Xi informal summit; subsequent diplomatic and civic convenings
- Golf course on property; historically hosted presidential and diplomatic guests

- Operates as a 501(c)(3) public benefit organization
- Admission to gardens is ticketed; visitor center is free
